Extremely fun facts about Sacramento:

  • Sacramento was the western endpoint of the Pony Express, the famous mail service that operated between Missouri and California from 1860 to 1861.
  • The historic district of Old Sacramento is built on top of raised streets and boardwalks, constructed in the 1860s to protect against flooding.
  • Sacramento is often referred to as the "City of Trees" because it has more trees per capita than most other major cities in the world, creating a lush urban forest.

Facts about Sacramento:

  1. The city's population: 524,943
  2. The year the city was incorporated: 1850
  3. The region of the state in which the city is located: Northern California
  4. The classification of the city: Urban
  5. The average income level of the city compared to the rest of the state: Lower
